About the Maulé Method

The Maulé method is a simplified evapotranspiration estimation approach that accounts for the limitations often encountered when using more complex methods with limited meteorological data.

Balanced Approach
Provides reasonable accuracy with moderate data requirements between simple and complex methods.
Data Requirements
Requires temperature and solar radiation data, with optional humidity corrections for improved accuracy.
Best Use Cases
Ideal for irrigation planning when you have basic weather station data but want better accuracy than temperature-only methods.
Calculation Method
Uses empirical coefficients with temperature and radiation: ET = k × (T_mean + a) × Rs × humidity_factor × range_factor

CSV Data Requirements

  • Required: Maximum temperature (Tmax), Minimum temperature (Tmin), Solar radiation (Rs)
  • Optional but recommended: Relative humidity (RH) for improved accuracy
  • Date column: Any column with 'date' in the name
  • Temperature units: Celsius degrees
  • Solar radiation units: MJ/m²/day
  • Humidity units: Percentage (%) if available
