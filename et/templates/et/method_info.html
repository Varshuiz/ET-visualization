ET Method Guide

Choose the right evapotranspiration method based on your available weather data

wb_sunny

Priestley-Taylor

A simplified energy balance method based on the assumption that under humid conditions, ET can be estimated using an empirical coefficient (α = 1.26) applied to equilibrium ET.

When to Use

  • Simple data requirements
  • Good accuracy in humid regions
  • Computationally efficient
  • Widely used in research

Watch-outs

  • Less accurate in arid regions
  • Does not account for wind effects
  • Assumes minimal advection

Required Data

  • Average air temperature (°C)
  • Solar radiation (MJ/m²/day)
ET₀ = α × (Δ/(Δ+γ)) × (Rn/λ), with daily G ≈ 0
air

Penman-Monteith

The FAO-56 international standard. Combines energy balance and aerodynamic terms to provide the most accurate ET₀ estimates for all climatic conditions.

When to Use

  • Highest accuracy — international standard
  • Valid across all climate types
  • Accounts for wind and humidity
  • FAO-56 endorsed method

Watch-outs

  • Requires the most input variables
  • Wind speed measurement needed
  • Sensitive to data quality

Required Data

  • Tmax, Tmin (°C)
  • Relative humidity (%)
  • Wind speed at 2m (m/s)
  • Solar radiation (MJ/m²/day)
ET₀ = [0.408Δ(Rn-G) + γ(900/(T+273))u₂(es-ea)] / [Δ + γ(1+0.34u₂)]
thermostat

Hargreaves-Samani

The simplest ET estimation approach requiring only Tmax and Tmin. Extraterrestrial radiation Ra is calculated internally from latitude and day of year.

When to Use

  • Only temperature data needed
  • Ideal for remote or historical data
  • Globally validated
  • Reliable long-term trend analysis

Watch-outs

  • Lower accuracy vs. PM in arid areas
  • Ignores humidity and wind effects
  • May overestimate in coastal regions

Required Data

  • Maximum temperature (°C)
  • Minimum temperature (°C)
ET₀ = 0.0023 × (Ra/λ) × (Tmax-Tmin)^0.5 × (Tavg+17.8)
agriculture

Maulé

A regionally calibrated method designed for prairie and semi-arid conditions. Uses an empirical coefficient tuned for the Canadian Prairies and similar climates.

When to Use

  • Calibrated for Prairie conditions
  • Minimal data requirements
  • Optional humidity correction
  • Good performance in semi-arid climates

Watch-outs

  • Region-specific — less tested globally
  • Less accurate outside Prairie climates
  • Empirical coefficient may need recalibration

Required Data

  • Tmax, Tmin (°C)
  • Solar radiation (MJ/m²/day)
  • Optional: Relative humidity (%)
ET₀ = k × (Tavg+a) × Rs × fRH × fΔT
