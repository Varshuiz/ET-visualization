ET Calculation Methods

Learn about the different evapotranspiration calculation methods available in this calculator

Priestley-Taylor

Overview

A simplified energy balance method that requires only temperature and solar radiation data. It's based on the assumption that under humid conditions with minimal advection, evapotranspiration can be estimated using an empirical coefficient (α = 1.26).

Advantages

  • Simple data requirements
  • Good accuracy in humid regions
  • Computational efficiency
  • Widely used in research

Limitations

  • Less accurate in arid regions
  • Doesn't account for wind effects
  • Assumes minimal advection

Required Data

  • Average air temperature (°C)
  • Solar radiation (MJ/m²/day)

Penman-Monteith

Overview

The FAO-56 international standard method for calculating reference evapotranspiration. It combines energy balance and aerodynamic terms to provide the most accurate ET₀ estimates for all climatic conditions.

Advantages

  • Highest accuracy
  • International standard (FAO-56)
