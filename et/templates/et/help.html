Help and Workflow Guide

Step-by-step instructions for loading weather data and using ET outputs

upload_file Data Upload Guide

CSV File Requirements

File Format:

  • CSV format (.csv extension)
  • Header row with column names
  • One record per day
  • No missing critical data points

Column Names (flexible):

  • Date: date, Date, DATE
  • Temperature: tmax and tmin (or max/min temperature columns)
  • Solar Radiation: solar, rad, radiation
  • Humidity: rh, humidity, relative_humidity
  • Wind: wind_speed, ws, or u2

Sample CSV Structure

Date,Tmax,Tmin,Solar_Radiation,Humidity,Wind_Speed 2024-01-01,25.5,14.3,18.2,65,2.1 2024-01-02,24.1,13.8,19.8,60,1.8 2024-01-03,21.3,10.9,16.5,70,2.4 ...

thunderstorm Forecast & Water Plan

  1. Visit the Forecast & Water Plan page
  2. Enter your city or town name in the search box
  3. Select your crop type from the dropdown
  4. Select your soil type from the dropdown
  5. Choose your forecast horizon (3 to 30 days)
  6. Click "Get Forecast"
  7. Review the daily forecast cards showing temperature, precipitation, and estimated ET
  8. Check the irrigation recommendation summary (Low / Moderate / High)
  9. Read the Irrigation Confidence Band chart - the grey band shows the P10–P90 range from historical years, the dashed line is the historical median, and the teal line is your current forecast. If the teal line runs near the top of the band, prepare for more irrigation than a typical year.

Note: Forecasts beyond 7 days are less reliable. Use the grey band and historical range as your guide rather than the single millimetre figure for extended horizons.

agriculture AquaCrop Simulation

AquaCrop is a crop water productivity model developed by the FAO. It simulates how crops grow and use water through the season, giving a more detailed picture of crop water demand than a simple ET calculation.

  1. Visit the Crop Simulation page
  2. Enter your location (city or township)
  3. Select your crop type (wheat, maize, etc.)
  4. Set your planting date and end date - use dates within the growing season (May 1–September 1 recommended for Alberta)
  5. Click "Run AquaCrop Simulation"
  6. Review the weekly output charts showing biomass, canopy cover, and water use
  7. Download the results if needed

Important: Dates must fall within the growing season. Simulations outside May–September for Alberta will produce no output. If you see "Simulation produced no output", check that your date range falls within the growing season and that your location has available weather data.

bar_chart Understanding Results

Statistics Explained

  • Average ET₀: Mean daily evapotranspiration
  • Maximum ET₀: Highest daily value
  • Minimum ET₀: Lowest daily value
  • Standard Deviation: Measure of variability

Chart Interpretation

  • Daily Values: Raw ET calculations
  • Smooth Line: 5-day rolling average
  • Seasonal Patterns: Look for trends
  • Peak Values: Summer maximums

warning Troubleshooting

File Upload Issues

  • Ensure file is in CSV format
  • Check that column headers match expected names
  • Verify no special characters in data
  • File size should be under 10MB

Calculation Errors

  • Check for missing required columns
  • Ensure numeric data is properly formatted
  • Remove any text in numeric columns
  • Verify date format (YYYY-MM-DD preferred)

Result Issues

  • Very high/low values may indicate data errors
  • Check units: temperature in °C, radiation in MJ/m²/day
  • Missing data points will be skipped
  • Compare results with local ET estimates

Credits & Data Sources

ET Methods & References

  • Penman-Monteith (FAO-56): Allen, R.G., Pereira, L.S., Raes, D., Smith, M. (1998). Crop evapotranspiration: Guidelines for computing crop water requirements. FAO Irrigation and Drainage Paper 56. https://www.fao.org/3/x0490e/x0490e00.htm

  • Hargreaves-Samani: Hargreaves, G.H. and Samani, Z.A. (1985). Reference crop evapotranspiration from temperature. Applied Engineering in Agriculture, 1(2), 96–99.
  • Maule: This implementation uses a project-calibrated Maulé-style ET formulation tuned for Prairie conditions (see method notes in code).

AquaCrop

  • AquaCrop model developed by Dirk Raes, Pasquale Steduto, Theodore C. Hsiao, and Elias Fereres (FAO, 2009).
  • FAO AquaCrop model: https://www.fao.org/aquacrop
  • AquaCrop Python package: https://github.com/aquacropos/aquacrop

Weather Data Sources

  • Historical & forecast weather: Open-Meteo Archive/Forecast APIs (https://open-meteo.com)
  • Canadian weather data (forecast/climate observations): Environment and Climate Change Canada (ECCC) via MSC Datamart and climate API endpoints (https://weather.gc.ca, https://dd.weather.gc.ca, https://api.weather.gc.ca)
