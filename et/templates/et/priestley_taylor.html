Priestley-Taylor Method

Simplified energy balance method for evapotranspiration calculation using temperature and solar radiation

About Priestley-Taylor Method

Key Features:

  • Simplified energy balance approach
  • Requires only temperature and solar radiation
  • Uses empirical coefficient (α = 1.26)
  • Good for humid regions with minimal advection

Required Data:

  • Average air temperature (°C)
  • Solar radiation (MJ/m²/day)
  • Optional: Date column for time series

Formula:

ET₀ = α × [Δ/(Δ + γ)] × (Rn/λ)

Where: α = 1.26 (empirical coefficient), Δ = slope of saturation vapor pressure curve, γ = psychrometric constant, Rn = net radiation, λ = latent heat of vaporization
